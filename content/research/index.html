I’m interested in high performance buildings that enhance occupant comfort and well-being. This includes indoor environmental quality, application of sensor technologies to built environments, psychophysics, and human thermal physiology & comfort. Below is an overview of some of the research I’ve done over the last 10 years.

Occupant surveys

I’m responsible for the CBE Occupant Survey, one of the largest and longest-running post-occupancy evaluation tools. This includes analysing the database of 90,000 responses from over 800 office buildings. I’ve published work summarising the survey and exploring the source of occupant dissatisfaction.

Much of my time on this project involves working closely with industry partners to evaluate their office buildings. Some of these organisations include International WELL Building Institute, Salesforce, JLL, Atelier Ten, Gensler, and Arup.

Wildfire resilience

Fine particulate matter is the largest environmental risk factor for human health and death in California, and wildfires in the western United States are a major source. This project will develop a technological intervention to help mitigate the health impacts of wildfire smoke in Californian homes. The solution includes software that programs smart thermostats to adjust ventilation system operation based on outdoor particulate concentrations to ensure adequate indoor air quality. The aim is to improve public health and climate resilience in residential buildings.

A core aim of the project is to evaluate the filtration efficiency of portable air cleaners compared to central furnace systems. This includes a mass balance model of particulate infiltration during the 2020 wildfires based on housing characteristics from census data at the tract level.
